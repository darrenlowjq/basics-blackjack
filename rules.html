Rules of this game

1. Begin by selecting whether the game is to be played in single or multi-player mode, and how many players will be playing if multi-player mode (max 5 players).

2. During each round, each player chooses how many chips they would like to bet before hands are dealt. Each player starts the game with 100 chips.

3. A player who drew a natural Blackjack wins 1.5x their bet (rounded up if bet is an odd number). A natural Blackjack consists of an Ace and one of the following: 10, Jack, Queen or King.

4. A player whose initial hand dealt consists of two cards of the same value (e.g. 3 of Hearts and 3 of Clubs), you have the option to split your hand to be played as 2 separate hands and get an extra card dealt to each hand. Note: You can only do this if you have enough chips to double up your bet with a second hand.

5. For all players who did not get a natural Blackjack, they will get opportunities to hit (draw extra card) or stand (not draw another card and let the turn move to next player if any).

6. Any player who hit and whose hand score exceeds 21 as a result busts and immediately loses their bet.

7. Once all players have either bust or chosen to stand, the dealer will draw their card(s). The dealer must draw until their hand is not less than 17 points. If the dealer busts as a result of drawing cards, each player wins 2x their bet.

8. If the dealer did not bust, the scores of all players who has chosen to stand will be compared against the dealer's hand. Players whose hands have a large score than the dealer wins 2x their bet. Players whose hands have a smaller score than the dealer loses their bet. Players whose hands have an equal score to the dealer neither wins any nor loses any chips.

9. At the point, all players who have run out of chips will be removed from the game. If there are no more players with any chips remaining, option will be provided to start an entirely new game. Otherwise, remaining players will be given the option to begin the next round by entering their bets.

A continuous tracker is provided to the right side of the screen showing the current state of the game. During play, all player's cards are visible but one of the dealer's cards is concealed and represented as the icon below. The dealer's concealed card is revealed during step 7 above.

Score Calculation of a given Hand

All numerical cards (2 to 10) provide points equal to their respective face value.

Jack, Queen and King are worth 10 points each.

An Ace can be either worth 1 point or 11 points.

A player busts when they have more than 21 points.

A Blackjack consists of an Ace and one of the following: 10, Jack, Queen or King (i.e. 21 points).
